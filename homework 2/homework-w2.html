(4) Read the text What Does the Metaverse Mean for the Future of Energy Consumption?. Write a brief analysis of the text based on the relation between data, energy and level of processes. From the energy perspective, is it possible to maintain this kind of technologies? Note: This is clearly not a right/wrong question, but a space to critically think about current issues related to the course.

Answer: Based on the text, the more data centres come online, the higher is electricity usage. New technologies and, using AI or VR require costly data processing, so developing and using them leads to more electricity usage. Such technologies require running many complicated processes, and they can't be stopped - everything must run smoothly without breaks, so technologies like the Metaverse may work properly. As mentioned in the text, technologies like the Metaverse could limit travel emissions, since people would be able to work without commuting and also people wouldn't take planes to go for concerts etc. But still, if the Metaverse is to use AI tools, these models are said to leave a big carbon footprint, so limitting travel emissions with increasing carbon footprint makes in the same time, it makes no sense. Moreover, if we want to globally use technologies like the Metaverse, we need to increase the level of processes, make more powerful infractructure. More power = more energy usage. As for the definition of Level of processes from the lecture notes, I would say that the Metaverse is classified into Level-N. From the energy perspective, I think maybe it is possible to maintain this kind of technologies, but not now, and not even in the near future. Even though there are many eco-friendly innovations visible, the world is far away from being 100% eco-friendly. In my opinion, now it's more important to increase renewable energy production in every country, and only then we can start thinking about this abstract projects like the Metaverse. If it needs very powerful computing infrastructe, we need to ensure that it can use renewable energy first.
